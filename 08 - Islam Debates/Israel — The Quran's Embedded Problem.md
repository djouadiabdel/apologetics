## The Argument in One Paragraph

The Quran uses the name Israel (*Isra'il*) 43 times and treats it as
Jacob's God-given title. Israel is a Hebrew word meaning "he who
wrestles with God" — given because Jacob physically encountered a
divine figure, wrestled him all night, saw him face to face, and
survived. The Quran's God either knew this meaning — in which case
he endorsed a story of God appearing in physical human form — or he
did not know it — in which case he is not all-knowing. There is no
third option. You cannot redefine the Hebrew root to escape this.
The word means what it means, the text explains why the name was
given, and the Quran uses it 43 times without correction.

## Step 1 — The Quran Claims Allah Is All-Knowing

This is the foundation. The Quran is explicit and repeated:

> "With Him are the keys of the unseen... He knows what is on land
> and in the sea." — Surah 6:59

> "You are the All-Knowing, the All-Wise." — Surah 2:32

> "And Allah has knowledge of all things." — Surah 2:282

> "He knows the unseen and the witnessed." — Surah 6:73

Allah knows the meaning of every word he uses. This is not
debatable within Islam. It is a foundational attribute of Allah
— *Al-Alim*, the All-Knowing.

**This means:** when Allah used the word *Isra'il* in the Quran,
he knew exactly what it meant.

## Step 2 — The Quran Uses Israel as Jacob's God-Given Title

The Quran does not merely mention Israel in passing. It:

- Uses *Isra'il* or *Bani Isra'il* **43 times** across multiple surahs
- Treats it explicitly as Jacob's legitimate God-recognized name
- Never corrects it, never redefines it, never offers an alternative meaning

The clearest endorsement is **Surah 3:93:**
> "All food was lawful to the Children of Israel (*Bani Isra'il*)
> except what **Israel** (*Isra'il*) made unlawful to himself before
> the Torah was revealed. Say: Bring the Torah and recite it,
> if you are truthful."

The Quran here:
1. Uses Israel as Jacob's personal name
2. Refers the reader **to the Torah itself** as an authority
3. Does this without correcting or redefining the name

Allah is not quoting a corrupted name reluctantly. He is using it
as a live title and pointing the reader to the Torah for context.

## Step 3 — What Israel Actually Means

The word **Israel** is Hebrew: **יִשְׂרָאֵל** (*Yisra'el*)

| Component | Hebrew | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| *Yisra-* | יִשְׂרָ | שָׂרָה (*sarah*) | to struggle, wrestle, strive, contend |
| *-el* | אֵל | אֵל | God |

**Israel = "He who wrestles with God" / "He who strives with God"**

This is not a Christian interpretation. It is not a later
commentary. It is not external scholarship imposed on the text.

**Genesis 32:28 gives the etymology inside the text itself:**
> "Your name will no longer be Jacob but Israel — **because** you
> have struggled (*sarita*) with God (*Elohim*) and with humans
> and have overcome."

The word *sarita* — "you have struggled" — is the verb form of
the same root as *Yisra*. The text is its own dictionary. The name
and its explanation use the same Hebrew root. This is called an
**etiological narrative** — a story that explicitly explains the
origin and meaning of a name. There is no stronger etymological
authority than the document that coined the word and explains it
in the same sentence.

## Step 5 — The Muslim Escape Attempts

### Attempt 1 — "Israel means servant of God"

This is the claim of Ibn Abbas and some Islamic scholars —
*Isra* = servant (*abd*), *il* = God.

**Why this fails completely:**

1. **Wrong root.** The Hebrew root שָׂרָה (*sarah*) means
   struggle/strive — not serve. This is not disputed in any
   Hebrew lexicon, dictionary, or linguistic source.

2. **Servant of God in Arabic is Abdullah** (*Abd* + *Allah*).
   That is what servant of God looks like. Israel does not
   follow that pattern.

3. **The Genesis text gives the etymology explicitly.** It does
   not say "you are called Israel because you serve God." It says
   "because you **struggled** (*sarita*) with God." The verb
   *sarita* shares the same root as *Yisra*. This is internal
   textual evidence that cannot be overridden by a medieval
   Arabic scholar's opinion.

4. **If Allah is all-knowing — he does not need Ibn Abbas to
   correct his vocabulary.** Allah knew the Hebrew root when
   he used the word. Ibn Abbas did not have access to Allah's
   knowledge. Allah did.

### Attempt 2 — "The Genesis story is corrupted Torah"

This is the standard Islamic escape hatch for inconvenient
Biblical texts.

**Why this closes the trap rather than opening it:**

> "You are now saying the Quran borrowed a name from a corrupted
> text — without correcting the corruption, without giving the
> real etymology, without telling Muslims what the name actually
> means. If the Torah was corrupted before Muhammad — why did
> Allah use a corrupted name 43 times without explanation?
> An all-knowing God corrects corruption. He does not silently
> endorse it 43 times."

Furthermore — Surah 3:93 does not say "the Torah has been
corrupted, ignore its account of this name." It says:
> "Bring the Torah and recite it, if you are truthful."

Allah refers the reader **to the Torah** as an authority on
this very topic. If the Torah is corrupted on this point —
Allah just directed his followers to a corrupted source
without warning them.

### Attempt 3 — "It was just a dream or vision, not a physical encounter"

Some Islamic commentators reinterpret Jacob's wrestling as a
dream to avoid the physical encounter.

**Why this fails:**

1. Genesis 32:24 — "So Jacob was left alone, and **a man wrestled
   with him** till daybreak." Not a vision. Not a dream. *A man.*

2. Genesis 32:25 — "When the man saw he could not overpower him,
   he touched the socket of Jacob's hip so that his hip was
   wrenched." Jacob's **hip was physically dislocated.** Dreams
   do not dislocate hips.

3. Genesis 32:31 — "The sun rose above him as he passed Penuel,
   and he was **limping** because of his hip." He woke up limping.
   A physical injury from a physical encounter.

4. The Quran does not describe this as a dream. It uses the name
   produced by the event without qualification.

## Step 6 — What the Wrestling Encounter Reveals

Genesis 32 describes a pattern that runs through the entire
Old Testament:

| Passage | Figure | Called |
|---|---|---|
| Genesis 32 | Wrestles Jacob | Man + God (*Elohim*) + Angel (*Hosea 12:4*) |
| Genesis 18 | Visits Abraham | Three men — one identified as YHWH |
| Exodus 3 | Speaks from burning bush | Angel of the LORD + YHWH interchangeably |
| Joshua 5:13-15 | Appears to Joshua | Commander of YHWH's army — receives worship |
| Judges 13 | Appears to Samson's parents | Man + Angel of the LORD + identified as God |

In every case: a figure who appears human, is called angel,
and is identified as God. This is not mythology. This is the
**systematic pattern** of divine-human encounter in the Hebrew
Bible — pointing toward the Incarnation as its ultimate
fulfillment.

The Quran borrows the name produced by the most famous of
these encounters — and uses it as the title of God's covenant
people — without realizing what it is endorsing.
