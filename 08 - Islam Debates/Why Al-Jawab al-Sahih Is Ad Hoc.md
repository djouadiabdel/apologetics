
Why Al-Jawab al-Sahih Is Ad Hoc

Ibn Taymiyya started with a fixed conclusion — the Bible cannot support the Trinity or divine Sonship because Islam is true — and then built explanations around every verse to protect that conclusion.

The test is simple:

    Would he have accepted these verses as evidence of divine Sonship if he had no prior commitment to Islam being true?

Proverbs 30:4 plainly asks for the name of God's Son in a cosmological context. Isaiah 9:6 plainly calls a coming child "Mighty God." Psalm 110 plainly has David calling the Messiah his Lord.

A neutral reader encounters these and asks — what do they mean?

Ibn Taymiyya encounters these and asks — how do I explain them away?

That is the difference. The conclusion was fixed first. The explanations followed. That is the definition of ad hoc.
The One Proof It Is Ad Hoc

He admitted the text is uncorrupted.

So the uncorrupted text contains these verses.

But the uncorrupted text's plain reading supports Christianity.

So he invented a reading that supports Islam — with no independent evidence that his reading is correct — purely to protect the conclusion he started with.

كاتب عاوز كدا — but the كاتب is Ibn Taymiyya, not God.
