## What the Argument Is

When a Christian or Jew points out that
Abraham, Moses, David, and Jesus never
identified as Muslims — never used the
word, never followed Islamic law, never
acknowledged Muhammad — the standard
Muslim response is:

> "That is because you misunderstand what
> Muslim means. Muslim simply means 'one
> who submits to God.' Abraham submitted
> to God. Moses submitted to God. Jesus
> submitted to God. Therefore they were
> all Muslims. Islam is not a new religion —
> it is the original religion of all the
> prophets, restored and completed by
> Muhammad."

This argument is presented as sophisticated
and inclusive. It is neither. It is a
semantic trick that collapses under basic
logical scrutiny — and worse, it creates
catastrophic internal contradictions for
Islam when its implications are followed
to their conclusions.

## Part 1 — The Definition Game

### What Muslims Mean by "Muslim"

The word *muslim* in Arabic is a participle
from the root *s-l-m* — meaning to submit,
to surrender, to be at peace. *Muslim* means
"one who submits" — specifically, one who
submits to Allah.

This is its meaning in the Quran:

**Surah 3:52:**
> قَالَ الْحَوَارِيُّونَ نَحْنُ أَنصَارُ اللَّهِ
> وَاشْهَدْ بِأَنَّا مُسْلِمُونَ
>
> "The disciples said: We are helpers of Allah.
> Bear witness that we are Muslims (*muslimoon*)."

**Surah 2:132:**
> وَوَصَّىٰ بِهَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بَنِيهِ وَيَعْقُوبُ
> يَا بَنِيَّ إِنَّ اللَّهَ اصْطَفَىٰ لَكُمُ
> الدِّينَ فَلَا تَمُوتُنَّ إِلَّا وَأَنتُم مُّسْلِمُونَ
>
> "And Abraham instructed his sons and Jacob:
> Indeed Allah has chosen for you this religion
> — so do not die except as Muslims."

The Muslim apologist uses the generic semantic
root — "one who submits to God" — to claim
every prophet who submitted to God was a Muslim.

### The Trick Being Performed

This is a **definitional expansion** — taking
a word that has a specific historical and
religious meaning and deliberately expanding
it to a generic meaning in order to absorb
figures who never belonged to the specific
category.

Let us make the trick visible with a parallel:

**The Christian version of the same trick:**
- "Christ" means "anointed one"
- David was anointed king of Israel
- Therefore David was a Christian

Every Christian would immediately recognize
this as wrong. David was anointed — but he
was not a Christian. Christianity refers to
a specific historical religious community
defined by faith in Jesus Christ as the
incarnate Son of God, his death and
resurrection, and the new covenant he
established.

Using the generic meaning of "anointed"
to claim David was a Christian is a
definitional trick. It works linguistically.
It is historically and theologically absurd.

The Muslim move is identical. Using the
generic meaning of "one who submits" to
claim Abraham, Moses, and Jesus were
Muslims is the same trick. It works
linguistically. It is historically and
theologically absurd.

### Why the Trick Does Not Work

A meaningful religious identity claim
requires more than etymological overlap.
It requires that the figure:

1. **Held the specific theological beliefs**
   of that religion
2. **Followed the specific practices**
   of that religion
3. **Identified with the specific community**
   of that religion
4. **Would have recognized themselves**
   in that religious category

Abraham, Moses, David, and Jesus fail all
four requirements for being Muslim — not
in the generic "submitter" sense, but in
the actual historical religious sense that
matters when we say "Muhammad was a Muslim."

## Part 2 — The Internal Contradictions

### Contradiction 1 — The Quran Presents Islam as New

If all prophets were already Muslims —
practicing the same religion — then Islam
is not new. It is a restoration of what
already existed.

But the Quran repeatedly presents the
Quranic revelation as something new —
something that the people needed to be
called toward as if they had departed from it:

**Surah 42:13:**
> شَرَعَ لَكُم مِّنَ الدِّينِ مَا وَصَّىٰ بِهِ
> نُوحًا وَالَّذِي أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ
> وَمَا وَصَّيْنَا بِهِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ
> وَعِيسَىٰ
>
> "He has ordained for you the same religion
> (*deen*) that He decreed for Noah, and what
> We revealed to you, and what We decreed
> for Abraham, Moses, and Jesus."

Notice: the Quran says the same *deen* was
ordained for all of them — but it was
ordained AS A DECREE, not as a continuous
practiced religion. The fact that it had
to be re-revealed to each prophet — and
re-revealed again to Muhammad — shows
it was not a continuous unbroken practice.

If all prophets were practicing Muslims
in an unbroken continuous religion — why
did Muhammad need to be sent? Why did the
message need to be restored? You do not
restore something that was never lost.

### Contradiction 2 — The Prophets' Teachings
Contradict Islam on Core Doctrines

This is where the argument becomes truly
indefensible. The Muslim claims Abraham,
Moses, and Jesus were Muslims. But the
actual teachings attributed to these
figures — in the very scriptures the
Quran endorses — contradict Islam on
every core point.

**Abraham:**

The Quran endorses the Torah of Moses
(Surah 3:3, 46:12). The Torah's account
of Abraham includes:
- YHWH appearing to Abraham as three men
  (Genesis 18) — a Trinitarian pre-figuring
  that Islam explicitly denies
- YHWH making an **eternal covenant** with
  Abraham through **Isaac** specifically —
  not Ishmael (Genesis 17:19-21)
- Abraham calling on the name YHWH —
  a specific divine name the Quran
  does not use for God and that encodes
  a personal covenant relationship foreign
  to Islamic theology

If Abraham was a Muslim — he was a very
confused one, whose God appeared in three
persons and made his covenant through
the wrong son.

**Moses:**

The Torah of Moses — which the Quran calls
a guide and mercy (Surah 46:12) — contains:

- A God who says he appeared to Abraham,
  Isaac, and Jacob as *El Shaddai* but
  is now revealing his personal name YHWH
  (Exodus 6:3) — a personal covenantal
  name with no equivalent in Islamic theology
- A covenant at Sinai that includes
  613 commandments — the vast majority
  of which Islam abrogates
- Laws about atonement through blood
  sacrifice (Leviticus 17:11) — a concept
  Islam explicitly rejects: Surah 6:164
  says no soul bears another's burden
- A Passover lamb whose blood protects
  from judgment — a clear substitutionary
  atonement typology that Islam denies

If Moses was a Muslim — he built an entire
sacrificial system around a theological
concept (substitutionary atonement) that
Islam says is false and pagan.

**Jesus:**

This is the most devastating case. The
Quran endorses the Gospel (Surah 5:46).
The Gospel — by any honest reading —
contains:

- Jesus claiming to be the Son of God
  (Mark 14:62, John 10:36) — a claim
  Islam says is the worst possible sin
  (*shirk*)
- Jesus saying "I and the Father are one"
  (John 10:30)
- Jesus saying "Before Abraham was,
  I AM" (John 8:58) — claiming the
  divine name of Exodus 3:14
- Jesus accepting worship (Matthew 28:9,
  John 20:28)
- Jesus instituting the Eucharist —
  his body and blood as atonement
- Jesus's disciples calling him Lord
  (*Kyrios*) — the Greek equivalent
  of YHWH in the Septuagint

The Muslim response is:
"Those verses were corrupted. Jesus
never said those things."

But this is the exact problem. The Muslim
says Jesus was a Muslim — and then is
forced to reject the entirety of what
Jesus is recorded as saying in the
Gospels the Quran endorsed.

Which leads directly to the Dawa Dilemma:
if the Gospels were corrupted — how
did Jesus's early disciples recognize
Muhammad's coming from corrupted texts?

### Contradiction 3 — The Quran Itself
Distinguishes the Religions

The Quran does not treat Judaism, Christianity,
and Islam as one continuous religion with
different names. It treats them as distinct
communities (*umam*) with distinct laws
(*sharia*):

**Surah 5:48:**
> لِكُلٍّ جَعَلْنَا مِنكُمْ شِرْعَةً
> وَمِنْهَاجًا
>
> "For each of you We have appointed a
> law (*shir'atan*) and a method (*minhajan*)."

A different law. A different method. For
each community. If they were all Muslims
practicing the same religion — why would
they need different laws and methods?

**Surah 2:62:**
> إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَالَّذِينَ هَادُوا
> وَالنَّصَارَىٰ وَالصَّابِئِينَ مَنْ آمَنَ
> بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَعَمِلَ
> صَالِحًا فَلَهُمْ أَجْرُهُمْ عِندَ رَبِّهِمْ
>
> "Indeed those who believed, and those
> who are Jews, and Christians, and Sabeans —
> whoever believed in Allah and the Last Day
> and did righteous deeds will have their
> reward with their Lord."

Notice: Jews and Christians are distinguished
as separate categories from "those who believed"
(Muslims). The Quran itself treats these as
distinct groups — not as one unified Muslim
community.

If all prophets were Muslim and their followers
were all practicing the same religion — this
verse makes no sense. You do not distinguish
between categories that are actually the same.

### Contradiction 4 — Abrogation Proves
the Religions Are Different

Islam has a doctrine of abrogation (*naskh*) —
later Quranic verses supersede earlier ones,
and Islamic law supersedes prior laws.

**Surah 2:106:**
> مَا نَنسَخْ مِنْ آيَةٍ أَوْ نُنسِهَا
> نَأْتِ بِخَيْرٍ مِّنْهَا أَوْ مِثْلِهَا
>
> "We do not abrogate a verse or cause it
> to be forgotten except that We bring
> one better than it or similar to it."

If all prophets were practicing the same
religion (Islam) — what exactly was being
abrogated? You cannot abrogate laws within
a single continuous religion without
acknowledging that the earlier version
was different from the later version —
meaning they were NOT the same religion.

The very existence of abrogation proves
that what Moses practiced was not the same
as what Muhammad practiced. The law changed.
The practices changed. The theology developed.
These are not the same religion with the
same name — they are different religious
systems that Islam is claiming retrospective
authority over.

## Part 3 — The Epistemological Problems

### Problem 1 — The Claim Is Unfalsifiable

If "Muslim" means "anyone who submits to God" —
then the claim "all prophets were Muslim"
becomes unfalsifiable. It cannot be tested.
It cannot be disproved. Any prophet who
submitted to any god in any religion could
be retroactively labeled a Muslim.

An unfalsifiable claim is not a theological
argument. It is a definitional trick. It
tells us nothing about reality — it just
rearranges words.

To make the claim meaningful — the Muslim
must define "Muslim" with enough specificity
to be falsifiable. But the moment they add
specificity — the claim becomes false.
A Muslim who:
- Follows the five pillars
- Believes Muhammad is the final prophet
- Does not associate partners with Allah
- Follows Islamic law

...is a category that none of the biblical
prophets fit. Not Abraham, not Moses,
not David, not Jesus.

### Problem 2 — The Joseph Smith Application

This is the same reductio from the Dawa
Dilemma — but applied here directly.

Joseph Smith taught that all the biblical
prophets were proto-Mormons. He said:
- The original religion of Adam was the
  same as what Joseph Smith restored
- Abraham, Moses, and the prophets held
  the Melchizedek priesthood
- The original gospel taught by Jesus
  was identical to Mormonism
- The corrupted churches lost this original
  religion and Smith restored it

The structure is letter-for-letter identical
to the Muslim claim:

| Muslim Claim | Mormon Claim |
|---|---|
| All prophets were Muslim | All prophets were proto-Mormon |
| Islam is the original religion | Mormonism is the restored original religion |
| Prior religions deviated from Islam | Prior religions deviated from the original gospel |
| Muhammad restored the original | Smith restored the original |
| "Muslim" means "one who submits" | "Saint" means "one who is holy" |

By what principle does the Muslim accept
their retroactive absorption of the prophets
and reject Smith's?

If the Muslim says "because the Quran is true" —
the Mormon says "because the Book of Mormon
is true." Both claims are circular.

If the Muslim says "because the prophets'
teachings cohere with Islam's core message
of monotheism" — the Mormon says "because
the prophets' teachings cohere with Mormonism's
core message of eternal progression."

There is no non-circular principle that
validates the Muslim move and invalidates
the Mormon move. The method itself is
the problem.

### Problem 3 — The Claim Weaponizes the
Prophets Against Their Own Testimony

The most dishonest aspect of the "all prophets
were Muslim" claim is that it forces the
prophets to testify against everything they
actually said.

Jesus said: "I and the Father are one."
The Muslim says: Jesus was a Muslim.
But a Muslim cannot say "I and the Father
are one" — that is *shirk*, the unforgivable sin.
Therefore: the Muslim must say Jesus never
said it.

But if Jesus never said it — the Gospel
is corrupted. But if the Gospel is corrupted —
the Quran's endorsement of the Gospel
as containing guidance and light (Surah 5:46)
is an endorsement of a corrupted text.
And if the Quran endorses corrupted texts
as guidance — the Quran is unreliable.

The Muslim claim about the prophets creates
a chain of implications that ends in the
Quran's own unreliability.

## Part 4 — The Theological Absurdities

### Absurdity 1 — Abraham the Muslim
Performed the Binding of Isaac

The Quran affirms that Abraham was willing
to sacrifice his son (Surah 37:102-107) —
though it substitutes Ishmael for Isaac.

But in the Torah — which the Quran endorses —
YHWH provides a ram as a substitute,
and Abraham names the place *YHWH Yireh* —
"YHWH will provide." This is a substitutionary
provision narrative — YHWH provides the
sacrifice — that the entire sacrificial
theology of the Torah builds upon, culminating
in Isaiah 53 and the suffering servant who
bears the sin of many.

If Abraham was a Muslim — he established
a narrative of substitutionary sacrifice
that Islamic theology explicitly denies.
He named the place after a God whose
name Islam does not use. He built a
typological framework that points directly
toward the death of Jesus — which Islam
calls a lie.

Abraham the Muslim inadvertently founded
Christian soteriology.

### Absurdity 2 — Moses the Muslim
Wrote About the Son of God

The Quran endorses the Torah. The Torah —
in its own plain reading — contains:

**Psalm 2:7** (Davidic):
> "You are my Son. Today I have begotten you."

**Proverbs 30:4:**
> "Who has gone up to heaven and come down?
> Who has gathered up the wind in his fists?
> Who has wrapped up the waters in a cloak?
> Who has established all the ends of the earth?
> What is his name, and **what is the name
> of his Son?** Tell me if you know!"

The Torah tradition itself asks for the
Son's name. If Moses was a Muslim — his
tradition was asking about the Son of a
God who, according to Islam, has no son.
(*Surah 112:3 — "He neither begets nor
is begotten."*)

The Muslim must either:
- Accept that the Torah (which the Quran
  endorses) contains Son of God language
  — which contradicts Islamic theology
- Say these passages were corrupted —
  which makes the Quran's endorsement
  of the Torah an endorsement of corrupted text

Both options are damaging.

### Absurdity 3 — Jesus the Muslim
Commanded His Disciples to Baptize
in the Trinitarian Name

**Matthew 28:19:**
> "Go therefore and make disciples of all
> nations, baptizing them in the name of
> the Father and of the Son and of the
> Holy Spirit."

The Muslim says Jesus was a Muslim.
A Muslim cannot command Trinitarian baptism.
Trinitarian baptism is *shirk* — the
association of partners with God —
the only unforgivable sin in Islam.

If Jesus was a Muslim and commanded
Trinitarian baptism — he commanded his
disciples to commit the only unforgivable
sin. Which makes Jesus the worst possible
teacher. Which contradicts the Quran's
presentation of Jesus as a noble prophet.

The Muslim must say: Jesus never said this.
Matthew 28:19 is corrupted.

But Matthew 28:19 is one of the most
well-attested verses in the entire New
Testament tradition — found in every
manuscript, cited by early church fathers
before any alleged corruption, quoted in
the Didache (a document dating to ~100 AD).

To call Matthew 28:19 a late corruption
requires dismissing the entire manuscript
tradition of the New Testament — which
the Muslim cannot do without also
dismissing the Quran's endorsement of
the Gospel as intact guidance.

## Part 5 — The Self-Defeating Conclusion

The "all prophets were Muslim" argument,
when followed to its logical conclusions,
produces the following results:

**Result 1 — It strengthens the Islamic Dilemma:**
If all prophets were Muslim and their
scriptures contained the original Islamic
message — those scriptures should confirm
Islam when read straight. But they do not.
They contain substitutionary atonement,
Trinitarian language, Son of God claims,
the divine name YHWH, eternal Davidic
covenant, and Isaiah 53. If these are
corruptions — the Islamic Dilemma is restored:
the Quran confirms corrupted texts. If
they are authentic — Islam is contradicted
by the original prophetic religion.

**Result 2 — It makes Islam unfalsifiable and
therefore meaningless as a truth claim:**
A claim that cannot be falsified carries
no information. "All prophets were Muslim"
in the generic sense tells us nothing about
whether Muhammad's specific teachings are true.

**Result 3 — It retroactively conscripts
figures who explicitly taught the opposite
of Islam's core claims:**
Jesus taught the Sonship of God.
Moses built substitutionary atonement into
the foundation of Israelite religion.
David described a divine figure seated at
YHWH's right hand (Psalm 110).
Isaiah described God being pierced in first
person (Zechariah 12:10 connected to Isaiah's
servant).

Calling them Muslims does not change what
they said. It just forces the Muslim to
either explain why God's own prophets
spent their careers teaching what Islam
calls the worst possible sin — or to
corrupt the very texts the Quran endorsed.

**Result 4 — It validates every religious
tradition's retroactive absorption claim:**
Mormons do it. Bahais do it. Certain Hindu
universalists do it. Once you accept the
method — you have no principle to stop it.
Every later religion can claim all prior
prophets were proto-adherents of their
system by redefining terms broadly enough.
