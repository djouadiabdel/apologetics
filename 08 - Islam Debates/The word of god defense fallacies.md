Great question. Here are every fallacy they commit — named and explained simply.

***

## **Fallacy 1 — Eisegesis (Reading Into the Text)**

The Quran says: *"His Word — He cast it into Mary."*

The Asharites read: *"He cast a vehicle that carried the Word into Mary."*

The word *wasila* (vehicle/tool) does not appear anywhere in the verse. They **inserted a concept into the text** that the text does not contain — then defended their insertion as if it were Quranic.

This is the most basic hermeneutical fallacy: **reading your conclusion into the source instead of drawing your conclusion from it.**

***

## **Fallacy 2 — Special Pleading**

They apply the rule *"the Word of God is eternal and uncreated"* to the Quran — but suspend that exact same rule for Jesus — with no principled reason why.

Both are called *Kalimatu Allah* in the Quran. The exact same title. But:

- Quran = eternal uncreated Word ✅
- Jesus = just a vehicle, not the actual Word ❌

Why does the rule apply to one but not the other? They have no Quranic answer. They just need it to not apply to Jesus — so they say it does not. That is special pleading: **exempting a case from a rule without justification — purely to protect a desired conclusion.**

***

## **Fallacy 3 — Circular Reasoning**

Their logic:

> Jesus is not divine because he is just a vehicle for the Word.
> We know he is just a vehicle because he is not divine.

The conclusion (Jesus is not divine) is being used as a premise to support itself. They assume Jesus is merely human — then use that assumption to interpret the verse — then say the verse proves Jesus is merely human.

**They never prove Jesus is just a vehicle. They assume it — then build on the assumption.**

***

## **Fallacy 4 — The Cart Before the Horse (Temporal Incoherence)**

You spotted this yourself. Their sequence:

> Jesus was the vehicle that delivered the Word to Mary → which caused Mary to conceive → Jesus.

Jesus is used as the vehicle **before Jesus exists.** The vehicle predates its own creation. The result of the delivery is the delivery mechanism itself.

This is not technically a named logical fallacy — it is worse. It is a **direct logical contradiction.** The vehicle cannot exist before the event that creates it. The position is not just weak — it is incoherent on its own terms.

***

## **Fallacy 5 — Equivocation**

They use *kalima* (Word) in two completely different senses without telling you:

- When talking about the Quran: *kalima* = the eternal divine self-expression of God
- When talking about Jesus: *kalima* = a message God wanted to send to Mary

Same word. Two completely different meanings. Switched silently depending on which conclusion they need to protect.

**Equivocation:** using the same word in two different senses within the same argument — without acknowledging the shift — to make the argument appear consistent when it is not.

***

## **Fallacy 6 — Argument from Consequences**

Their underlying motivation — which they sometimes state openly — is:

> "If we accept that Jesus is the eternal Word, it leads to Christianity — which is wrong — therefore Jesus cannot be the eternal Word."

This is **argument from consequences:** rejecting a conclusion because you dislike where it leads — rather than showing the reasoning that produces it is actually flawed.

The consequences of a position being uncomfortable does not make the position false. It just makes it uncomfortable.
