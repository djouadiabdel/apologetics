## The Argument and Its Fallacies

The argument is actually **three separate fallacies stacked on top of each other**, not just equivocation. Let me break each one:

---

## Fallacy 1 — Equivocation (the one you spotted)

The word _tajri_ (runs/moves) is given a different meaning in every century depending on what science currently says:

- 8th century: means geocentric orbit
    
- 21st century: means galactic solar motion
    
- 22nd century: will mean whatever science discovers next
    

This is equivocation — using the same word to mean completely different things depending on convenience. The word is not carrying a stable meaning across time. It is being emptied of content and refilled with whatever is currently fashionable.

---

## Fallacy 2 — Unfalsifiability

If the Quran can mean whatever current science says — it can **never be wrong**. And a claim that can never be wrong carries **zero information**.

Think about it: what scientific discovery would falsify this verse? Name one. There is none — because the method guarantees the verse will always be reinterpreted to fit whatever is discovered.

This is not a feature. It is a fatal flaw. It means the Quran is not describing or predicting anything. It is a blank canvas onto which science paints its current picture. A canvas is not revelation.

---

## Fallacy 3 — Post-Hoc Retrofitting

The impressive claim would be: **the Quran described galactic solar motion in 632 AD before science discovered it in the 20th century.**

But that is NOT what Shahrour is saying. He is saying: science discovers galactic solar motion → THEN Muslims reinterpret the Quran to match it.

The discovery comes first. The reinterpretation comes after. This is not prediction. This is reading a conclusion backwards into a text that already exists.

You can do this with **any ancient text**. Homer's Odyssey describes the sea in ways that can be reinterpreted using fluid dynamics. The Vedas describe fire in ways that can be reinterpreted using thermodynamics. Shahrour's method, if valid, proves every ancient text is divinely inspired. Which proves nothing.

---

## Fallacy 4 — The 8th Century Reader Was WRONG

This one is the most devastating and nobody says it clearly enough.

Shahrour admits the 8th century reader interpreted the verse as geocentrism — the sun orbiting the earth. Geocentrism is **false**.

So the Quran — the eternal word of God, clear guidance for all humanity — **permitted systematic false belief for over 1,000 years**. Every Muslim from 632 AD to roughly the 17th century read this verse and believed something scientifically false because of it.

A truly clear divine text does not mislead its readers for ten centuries and then congratulate itself for being reinterpretable. If the verse meant galactic solar motion all along — Allah failed to communicate that to every reader for a millennium.

---

## Fallacy 5 — Shahrour's Method Destroys Islamic Jurisprudence

If meaning is mobile — if interpretation changes every century as knowledge accumulates — then:

- The hudud punishments can be reinterpreted when modern penology advances
    
- The marriage laws can be reinterpreted when gender studies advances
    
- The inheritance laws can be reinterpreted when economics advances
    
- The dietary laws can be reinterpreted when nutrition science advances
    

Every classical Islamic scholar would reject this immediately. The Quran cannot simultaneously be the unchanging foundation of sharia AND have mobile content that changes every century. Shahrour's principle, applied consistently, dissolves Islamic law entirely. Most Muslim scholars consider him a heretic for exactly this reason.

---

## Addition 1 — The Quran's Own Self-Description Contradicts This

The Quran describes itself as _mubeen_ — clear — **19 times**:

**Surah 12:1:**

> تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ الْمُبِينِ  
> "These are the verses of the clear Book."

**Surah 54:17:**

> وَلَقَدْ يَسَّرْنَا الْقُرْآنَ لِلذِّكْرِ  
> "And We have made the Quran easy to remember and understand."

If the Quran's meaning requires 21st century astrophysics to understand correctly — it is the **most obscure document ever written**, not the clearest. It demands knowledge that did not exist for 1,400 years before it can be properly read.

Allah told 7th century Arabs that his book was clear and easy. Shahrour is telling them it was actually incomprehensible until NASA was founded.

---

## Addition 2 — The Intended Audience Problem

The Quran was revealed to 7th century Arabs in their own language. The immediate audience — the Companions, the Sahabah — are considered by Islamic tradition the **gold standard of Quranic understanding**. Their interpretation is the most authoritative in Islamic jurisprudence.

If the Companions understood _tajri_ as geocentric motion — and they were wrong — then:

- Either the gold standard of Quranic interpretation produced systematic scientific error
    
- Or the gold standard is not actually the standard
    

Both options are catastrophic for Islamic epistemology. You cannot simultaneously say the Companions' understanding is authoritative AND that their understanding of this verse was wrong for 1,000 years.

---

## Addition 3 — The Verse Doesn't Actually Fit the New Science Either

This is rarely noticed. Look at the full verse:

**Surah 36:38:**

> وَالشَّمْسُ تَجْرِي لِمُسْتَقَرٍّ لَّهَا  
> "The sun runs to **a resting place** (_mustaqarrin_) of its own."

_Mustaqarr_ means a place of settling, a fixed resting point — a destination at which motion terminates.

In the modern galactic motion interpretation, the sun is moving toward the **solar apex** near the Hercules constellation. But the sun has **no resting place**. It is in perpetual orbital motion around the galactic center — it will never arrive and stop. It has no _mustaqarr_.

So even the reinterpretation fails on the verse's own terms. The scientific reality the Muslim apologist is trying to import does not actually satisfy what the verse says. The verse requires a resting destination. Modern astronomy provides perpetual motion.

---

## Addition 4 — The Progressive Revelation Problem

Shahrour's principle implies that the Quran's meaning in 2026 is **richer and more correct** than its meaning in 632 AD. Understanding grows as science grows.

This means:

- In 632 AD — the Quran was least understood
    
- In 2026 — it is better understood
    
- In 2200 — it will be even better understood
    

This is the **inverse of revelation**. Revelation is knowledge flowing DOWN from God to man — from complete to incomplete, from perfect to partial, from the infinite to the finite. Shahrour's model has knowledge flowing UP — from man to the Quran — as human science improves the text's meaning.

This is not divine revelation. It is a human document that gets smarter as humans get smarter. Which is exactly what you would expect from a human document — and the opposite of what you expect from God.

---

## Addition 5 — The Self-Refutation Test

Ask Shahrour's principle about itself:

> "The principle of stability of text and mobility of content — is THIS principle also subject to mobility of content?"

If yes — the principle itself will mean something different in 2100. It is unstable. We cannot rely on it now.

If no — it is the one fixed meaning in the entire Quran. Everything else is mobile but this meta-principle is frozen. Why? What justifies freezing this one claim while everything else moves? Special pleading.

The principle cannot account for itself. It is self-refuting.

---

## Addition 6 — The Divine Communicator Problem

This goes deeper than Fallacy 4. Frame it as a dilemma about God:

Allah knew the correct meaning of _tajri_ — galactic solar motion. He chose to communicate it in a way that **every single reader for 1,000+ years** understood it to mean something false.

This means either:

- **Allah could not** communicate the correct meaning to 7th century Arabs — he lacked the communicative capacity. But omnipotence includes communicative omnipotence.
    
- **Allah chose not to** communicate the correct meaning — he intentionally allowed his readers to hold false scientific beliefs. But a God of truth does not deliberately cultivate false belief in his worshippers.
    
- **The verse does not mean** galactic solar motion — and the modern reinterpretation is being imposed onto the text from outside.
    

The third option is the honest one. The first two impugn Allah's competence or honesty.

---

## Addition 7 — The Slippery Slope Is Not a Fallacy Here

Normally "slippery slope" is a fallacy. Here it is not — because Shahrour himself applies this principle to **legal verses**, not just scientific ones. He explicitly uses mobile content to argue for reinterpreting hudud punishments, gender laws, and inheritance.

This means the slippery slope already happened. Shahrour already slid to the bottom. And mainstream Islamic scholars already declared him a heretic for it. Which means:

- **If you accept his method** — you end up with Shahrour's liberal Islam that dissolves sharia
    
- **If you reject his method** — you cannot use it for the scientific verses either
    

You cannot use the method selectively — keeping it for science apologetics but blocking it at sharia. The method is either valid or it is not. If it is valid — Islamic law is negotiable. If it is not valid — the science apologetics collapses.
