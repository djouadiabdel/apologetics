**Surah 21:105:**

> وَلَقَدۡ كَتَبۡنَا فِي ٱلزَّبُورِ مِنۢ بَعۡدِ ٱلذِّكۡرِ  
> "And We have already written in the **Psalms** (_Zabur_)  
> **after the reminder** (_zikr_)..."

## The Chronological Proof

The argument is purely internal to the Quran — no outside knowledge needed:

## The Corroborating Proof — Surah 21:48

You do not even need the chronological argument alone —  
because the Quran confirms it directly one verse earlier  
in the same surah:

**Surah 21:48:**

> وَلَقَدۡ ءَاتَيۡنَا مُوسَىٰ وَهَٰرُونَ ٱلۡفُرۡقَانَ  
> وَضِيَآءٗ وَذِكۡرٗا لِّلۡمُتَّقِينَ
> 
> "And We gave Moses and Aaron the criterion and a light  
> and a **zikr** for the righteous."

Moses received the zikr. Moses received the Torah.  
Therefore the zikr = Torah. This is not inference —  
this is the Quran explicitly naming Moses as the  
recipient of the zikr in the same chapter.

## Why This Matters — The Trap It Creates

Now go back to **Surah 15:9:**

> إِنَّا نَحۡنُ نَزَّلۡنَا ٱلذِّكۡرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَٰفِظُونَ
> 
> "Indeed We sent down the zikr and indeed We will be its guardian."

Muslims quote this verse to say the Quran is protected.  
But the Quran itself just proved — in Surah 21 — that  
the zikr was given to Moses and existed before the Psalms.

So **Surah 15:9 promises Allah will protect the Torah.**

If the Torah was corrupted — Allah broke his own promise.  
If Allah did not break his promise — the Torah is preserved.  
Either way, the corruption claim is destroyed by the  
Quran itself, using nothing but its own verses.
