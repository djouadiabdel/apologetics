## Definition 1 — Corruption (Tahrif)
### What the Word Means

The Arabic word is *tahrif* — from the root
*ha-ra-fa* — meaning to deviate, to distort,
to alter from its original form. The Quran
uses this word and related terms in several
places to accuse Jews of distorting their
scriptures:

**Surah 4:46:**
> مِّنَ الَّذِينَ هَادُوا يُحَرِّفُونَ الْكَلِمَ
> عَن مَّوَاضِعِهِ
>
> "Among the Jews are those who distort
> (*yuharrifoona*) words from their places."

**Surah 5:13:**
> فَبِمَا نَقْضِهِم مِّيثَاقَهُمْ لَعَنَّاهُمْ
> وَجَعَلْنَا قُلُوبَهُمْ قَاسِيَةً ۖ يُحَرِّفُونَ
> الْكَلِمَ عَن مَّوَاضِعِهِ
>
> "Because of their breaking of their covenant,
> We cursed them and made their hearts hard.
> They distort words from their places."

Note what the Quran does NOT say here. It does
not say the physical manuscripts were replaced.
It says words were distorted *from their places*
— *an mawadihi* — meaning the words were
misplaced, misquoted, misread, or
misinterpreted.

This is actually a significant internal problem
for the modern Dawah position. Classical Muslim
scholars debated whether *tahrif* meant:
- *Tahrif al-lafz* — corruption of the actual
  text, the physical words on the page
- *Tahrif al-ma'na* — corruption of meaning,
  deliberate misinterpretation of intact text

**Ibn Khaldun, Ibn Hazm, and many classical
scholars argued for *tahrif al-ma'na*** — the
text is intact, the Jews misread it. This
position actually strengthens the Islamic
Dilemma because it means the Torah in hand
is reliable — and it contradicts the Quran.

**Modern Dawah shifted to *tahrif al-lafz*** —
the physical text was changed — precisely to
escape the Islamic Dilemma. But this escape,
as we will show, creates the worse problem
we are now examining.

### What Corruption Logically Entails

This is the point that cannot be avoided.
The moment you say a text is corrupted —
any version of corrupted — the following
consequences are logically inescapable:

**Consequence 1 — Unreliability Without Sorting:**
If a text contains both authentic divine
revelation AND human fabrication, you cannot
read it straight. Every sentence is potentially
authentic or potentially fabricated. You do
not know which is which just by looking at
the text — because the fabricated parts were
designed to look like the authentic parts.
That is what makes them corrupted rather
than just obviously wrong.

This means **pointing to the text proves nothing
on its own.** The Muslim who says "the Torah
was corrupted" cannot then say "and here is
what the authentic Torah said" by simply
quoting from the corrupted Torah. Every
quote he makes is drawn from a text he has
already declared unreliable.

**Consequence 2 — A Sorting Mechanism Is Required:**
To use any part of a corrupted text you
need a criterion — a principled method —
to sort authentic from fabricated content.
This criterion must be:
- *External* to the text itself (because the
  text is corrupted — you cannot use a
  corrupted text to verify its own authentic
  parts)
- *Non-circular* (it cannot assume the truth
  of the conclusion it is verifying)
- *Publicly accessible* (it must have been
  available to the people being held responsible
  for using it)
- *Sufficiently discriminating* (it must be
  precise enough to actually distinguish
  authentic from corrupted content — not just
  vague intuition)

This is not a philosophical imposition from
outside Islam. It is what the word corruption
logically requires. Any Muslim who denies
Premise 1 — that corruption requires a sorting
criterion — is claiming that a text can be
simultaneously corrupted AND reliably readable
without any external verification method.
That is a contradiction. It cannot be maintained
without special pleading.

### The Three Versions of Corruption — All Closed

Muslim apologists sometimes try to find a
middle ground on corruption that avoids the
sorting criterion problem. There are three
versions they attempt:

---

**Version A — Total Corruption**

*Claim:* The entire Torah and Gospel as they
exist today are fabrications. Nothing in
them reflects the original divine revelation.

*Problem 1:* The Quran explicitly confirms
the Torah and Gospel as divine revelation
in multiple places:

**Surah 3:3:**
> نَزَّلَ عَلَيْكَ الْكِتَابَ بِالْحَقِّ مُصَدِّقًا
> لِّمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ وَأَنزَلَ التَّوْرَاةَ
> وَالْإِنجِيلَ
>
> "He sent down the Book in truth, confirming
> what was before it. And He sent down the Torah
> and the Gospel."

**Surah 5:46:**
> وَقَفَّيْنَا عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِم بِعِيسَى ابْنِ
> مَرْيَمَ مُصَدِّقًا لِّمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ
> مِنَ التَّوْرَاةِ ۖ وَآتَيْنَاهُ الْإِنجِيلَ
> فِيهِ هُدًى وَنُورٌ
>
> "We sent Jesus son of Mary confirming the Torah
> before him. And We gave him the Gospel — in it
> is guidance and light."

If the Torah and Gospel are total fabrications —
the Quran is confirming fabrications. Which means
the Quran is either wrong or deliberately
misleading. Total corruption destroys the Quran's
own credibility.

*Problem 2:* The Quran tells Muhammad to verify
himself from the prior scriptures (Surah 10:94).
You do not send someone to a total fabrication
for verification. Total corruption is
self-defeating.

---

**Version B — Partial Corruption**

*Claim:* Some specific parts of the Torah
and Gospel were corrupted — altered or removed —
but large portions remain authentic.

*This is the modern Dawah position.*

*Problem:* This version still requires a
sorting criterion. In fact it requires a
*more* sophisticated one than total corruption
— because now you have a mixed document where
authentic and fabricated content are
interleaved. To use any part of this document
you need a principled method to classify each
section as authentic or corrupted.

The Muslim will often say: "We use the Quran
to sort." But this is circular — the Quran's
authority is what is being verified by the
Torah. We will address this fully under Premise 6.

---

**Version C — Selective Corruption**

*Claim:* The Torah was corrupted specifically
in the places that mentioned Muhammad —
those passages were deliberately removed or
altered by Jewish scribes who wanted to
conceal his coming.

*This seems clever. It is not.*

*Problem 1 — Circularity:*
How do you know which passages mentioned
Muhammad? You know a passage mentioned Muhammad
because Muhammad is not there — but should be.
How do you know he should be there? Because
he is a prophet described in the Torah. How
do you know he is described in the Torah?
Because the passages that described him were
removed.

This is a closed circular argument. You are
using the conclusion — Muhammad was in the
Torah — to identify the evidence — which
passages were removed — to prove the conclusion
— Muhammad was in the Torah.

*Problem 2 — The Factive Language Problem:*
Surah 2:146 says Jews and Christians recognize
Muhammad "as they recognize their own children."
This is not a description of people who vaguely
sense that a prophet is missing from their
corrupted texts. This is a description of
certain, direct, immediate recognition — like
recognizing your own child's face.

If the passages about Muhammad were removed —
how are they recognizing him with this
certainty? What are they recognizing him FROM?
A corrupted text with Muhammad's sections
deleted cannot produce the recognition
certainty the Quran claims they had.

*Problem 3 — The Concealment Problem:*
Surah 2:159 says they concealed clear proofs
and guidance. You cannot conceal what was
already removed. If the passages about Muhammad
were deleted by earlier scribes — the Jews
and Christians at the time of Muhammad had
nothing to conceal.

## Definition 2 — General Confirmation Plus Conscious Editing

### The Modern Dawah Escape

When confronted with the Islamic Dilemma —
the argument that the Quran confirms a Torah
that contradicts the Quran — modern Muslim
apologists developed a refined position:

> "The Quran does not fully confirm the Torah
> and Gospel. It generally confirms the true
> parts and consciously edits the corrupted
> or falsified parts. Therefore the Quran
> agreeing with most of the Torah while
> contradicting specific points is exactly
> what we would expect."

This sounds sophisticated. It sounds like
it threads the needle between total
confirmation and total rejection. And on the
surface it appears to dissolve the Islamic
Dilemma entirely.

It does not. It transforms it into something
worse.

### What This Position Actually Claims

Let us be precise about what "generally
confirms and consciously edits" means.
It makes the following claims simultaneously:

**Claim A:** The Torah contains authentic
divine revelation that the Quran affirms.

**Claim B:** The Torah also contains corrupted
human fabrication that the Quran corrects.

**Claim C:** The Quran is the reliable
mechanism that sorts authentic from fabricated
content — keeping what is true, editing
what is false.

**Claim D:** Despite this corruption, Muhammad
is still written in the Torah.

**Claim E:** Jews and Christians could still
recognize Muhammad from this corrupted Torah.

**Claim F:** They were still morally blameworthy
for rejecting him on the basis of that Torah.

Now look at what happens when you hold all
six claims simultaneously.

Claim C says the Quran sorts the Torah.
But Claims D, E, and F require that Jews and
Christians — who did not have the Quran —
could use the Torah to verify Muhammad's
prophethood. If the Quran is the sorting
mechanism, and Jews and Christians did not
have the Quran, then Jews and Christians
had no sorting mechanism. And if they had
no sorting mechanism, they had no reliable
access to the authentic parts of the Torah.
And if they had no reliable access to the
authentic parts — they could not have
recognized Muhammad, could not have found
him written there, and cannot be justly
blamed for failing to do so.

Claims C, D, E, and F cannot all be true
at the same time. The Muslim must choose.
And every choice they make destroys something
essential to Islam.

### The Quran as Editor — A Self-Defeating Position

There is a deeper problem with Claim C that
deserves its own treatment.

If the Quran is consciously editing the Torah —
that means the Quran is making editorial
decisions about which parts of the Torah
to keep and which parts to revise. This
requires that the Quran has independent
access to the original uncorrupted revelation
to compare against the corrupted Torah.

Fine. But then two questions arise:

**Question 1:** How do we know the Quran's
editorial decisions are correct?

The Muslim will say: "Because the Quran is
divine revelation." But this is exactly the
claim under dispute. You cannot use the
Quran's reliability to establish the Quran's
reliability. That is the definition of
circular reasoning.

**Question 2:** If the Quran has access to
the original uncorrupted Torah, why did
Allah not simply restore the original
instead of consciously editing a corrupted
version?

The Muslim has no satisfying answer to this.
The most common response is: "Because the
Quran is not a restoration of the Torah —
it is a new final revelation." But this
creates a new problem: if the Quran is not
a restoration of the Torah but an independent
new revelation — then the Quran's confirmation
of the Torah is not evidence that the Torah
is true. It is just the Quran making claims
about itself. Which again is circular.

### What This Definition Locks In

By the end of Definition 2, the following
is established:

- The general confirmation plus conscious
  editing model requires a non-circular
  external sorting criterion
- The Quran cannot serve as that criterion
  for people evaluating the Quran
- The model creates a conflict between
  Claims C, D, E, and F that cannot be
  resolved without circularity
- The model is not a solution to the
  Islamic Dilemma — it is a more complex
  version of the same problem

## Definition 3 — Factive Mental States

### Why This Definition Is the Sharpest Blade in the Argument

Most arguments against Islam operate from
outside — using external evidence, historical
criticism, textual analysis. This definition
allows us to do something different: show
that the Quran's own words require a state
of affairs that the Quran's own corruption
claim makes impossible.

The Quran does not just claim Muhammad is
somehow implied by the prior scriptures.
It uses specific, precise, epistemically
loaded language. That language matters.

### What a Factive Mental State Is

In philosophy of language and epistemology,
a factive mental state is a mental state
whose accurate attribution to a person
logically entails the truth of its content.

The clearest example is knowledge:
- "John knows it is raining" is only true
  if it actually is raining. If it is not
  raining, John does not know it is raining —
  at best he falsely believes it is raining.
  Knowledge, by definition, cannot have a
  false object.

Other factive states:
- *Remember* — "She remembers locking the
  door" entails she actually locked the door.
  If she did not lock it, she does not remember
  doing so — she misremembers.
- *Recognize* — "He recognized the face"
  entails the face was actually there and
  actually was the face of the person he
  identified. You cannot recognize someone
  who is not present. You can mistake someone
  for them — but that is not recognition.
- *Discover* — "They discovered the error"
  entails there actually was an error. If
  there was no error, they did not discover
  one — they imagined one.
- *Conceal* — "She concealed the evidence"
  entails the evidence actually existed and
  was accessible to her. You cannot conceal
  what does not exist. You cannot conceal
  what you cannot access.

### How the Quran Uses These States

The Quran uses factive mental state language
throughout its case against Jews and Christians.
This is not accidental. It is precisely how
the Quran constructs its moral case against
them — their culpability rests entirely on
these factive knowledge claims.

**Surah 7:157:**
> الَّذِي يَجِدُونَهُ مَكْتُوبًا عِندَهُمْ
> فِي التَّوْرَاةِ وَالْإِنجِيلِ
>
> "Whom they **find** (*yajidoonahu*) **written**
> (*maktooban*) **with them** (*indahum*)
> in the Torah and the Gospel."

Unpack every word:
- *Yajidoonahu* — they find him, present tense,
  active. Not "they could find him if the text
  were uncorrupted." Not "they would have found
  him in the original." They find him — now —
  in the text in their hands.
- *Maktooban* — written. A specific textual
  claim. Not a vague spiritual impression.
  Written. In text. Locatable.
- *Indahum* — with them. In their possession.
  In the text they are currently holding.

This verse does not describe vague prophetic
expectation. It describes current, active,
textual presence — findable by anyone reading
the Torah and Gospel in the time of Muhammad.

**Surah 2:146:**
> يَعْرِفُونَهُ كَمَا يَعْرِفُونَ أَبْنَاءَهُمْ
>
> "They **recognize** him (*ya'rifoonahu*)
> as they recognize their own sons."

*Ya'rifoonahu* — from *'arafa* — to know with
familiarity, to recognize, to identify with
certainty. This is not the word for belief
(*aamana*) or conjecture (*zanna*) or hope
(*raja*). This is direct, certain, immediate
recognition — the same cognitive state as
looking at your child's face and knowing
who they are.

The comparison "as they recognize their own
sons" is doing important work here. You do
not recognize your own son by:
- Probabilistic reasoning
- Vague textual clues
- Circular inference

You recognize your own son by direct, unmediated,
certain identification. The Quran is claiming
that Jewish and Christian scholars had THIS
QUALITY of recognition of Muhammad from their
scriptures.

**Surah 2:159:**
> إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَكْتُمُونَ مَا أَنزَلْنَا
> مِنَ الْبَيِّنَاتِ وَالْهُدَىٰ
>
> "Indeed those who **conceal** (*yaktumoon*)
> what We sent down of clear proofs
> and guidance..."

*Yaktumoon* — they conceal, they hide, they
suppress. This is an active verb describing
deliberate suppression of something that:
- Actually exists
- Is accessible to the one concealing it
- Is clear enough to be identified as
  *bayyinat* — clear proofs — and *huda* —
  guidance

You cannot conceal what does not exist.
You cannot conceal what you cannot access.
You cannot conceal what is so obscured by
corruption that you yourself cannot find it.

### The Implication That Cannot Be Escaped

Put these three verses together and the
logical picture is devastating for the
corruption claim:

1. Muhammad is *written in the text they
   currently hold* (7:157)
2. They *recognize him with certainty*
   from that text (2:146)
3. They *actively conceal* this clear
   knowledge (2:159)

For all three factive states to be
simultaneously true:
- The text must contain Muhammad's description
  in a findable, readable form
- That description must be clear enough to
  produce certain recognition
- That recognition must be accessible enough
  to be something worth concealing

Now introduce corruption.

If the text is corrupted — if the passages
about Muhammad were altered, removed, or
obscured — then:
- You cannot *find* what has been removed
- You cannot *recognize with certainty* what
  has been obscured
- You cannot *conceal* clear proofs that
  are no longer clear

All three factive states become impossible
under the corruption model. The Quran's
own language requires the text to be in
a state that the Quran's own corruption
claim says it is not in.

### The Muslim Escape Attempt — and Why It Fails

A Muslim apologist will often respond:
> "The recognition was not from the corrupted
> text. It was from oral tradition, or from
> partial surviving authentic passages,
> or from the scholars who preserved the
> original teaching even if the text was
> corrupted."

This response actually undermines the
Quran's case, not strengthens it:

**Against oral tradition:**
Surah 7:157 specifically says *maktooban* —
written. Not spoken. Not transmitted orally.
Written. In the Torah and Gospel. The claim
is about written textual presence. Oral
tradition cannot satisfy a written textual
claim.

**Against partial authentic passages:**
If recognition came from partial authentic
passages — how did Jewish and Christian
scholars know those specific passages were
authentic and not also corrupted? They needed
a sorting criterion. We are back to the
same problem.

**Against scholarly preservation:**
If the scholars preserved the original
teaching outside the corrupted text —
why does the Quran say the text itself
(*al-kitab*) contains Muhammad? And why
are those same scholars condemned for
concealment? If they privately preserved
the authentic teaching, they had not
lost access to it — they simply refused
to publish it. But then the corruption
of the written text is irrelevant to
their culpability. Their culpability is
about private knowledge, not textual
recognition. And private knowledge
cannot satisfy the public written claim
of 7:157.

Every escape collapses. The factive
language of the Quran requires textual
integrity. The corruption claim destroys
textual integrity. They cannot both be true.

## Definition 4 — Epistemic Access

### What the Term Means — and Why It Is Not an External Imposition

Epistemic access is the cognitive and
evidential capacity to evaluate a claim —
to have the means, the evidence, and the
reasoning tools necessary to reach justified
belief about whether that claim is true or false.

A person has epistemic access to a claim if:
- The relevant evidence is available to them
- The evidence is interpretable by them
- The evidence is sufficient to ground justified
  belief, not just bare possibility

A person lacks epistemic access when:
- The evidence does not reach them
- The evidence is present but uninterpretable
- The evidence is corrupted beyond reliable use

### Why This Is the Quran's Own Standard — Not an External One

The single most common Muslim objection to this
argument is:
> "You are importing Western epistemological
> standards into Islam. In Islam, the standard
> for culpability is different."

This objection fails for a simple reason: the
Quran itself establishes epistemic access as
the standard for culpability — in multiple places.

**Surah 4:165:**
> رُّسُلًا مُّبَشِّرِينَ وَمُنذِرِينَ لِئَلَّا
> يَكُونَ لِلنَّاسِ عَلَى اللَّهِ حُجَّةٌ
> بَعْدَ الرُّسُلِ
>
> "Messengers as bearers of good news and
> as warners — so that people would have
> no argument (*hujja*) against Allah
> after the messengers."

The Quran explicitly says the purpose of
sending messengers is to remove the *hujja* —
the legitimate excuse — from people. This
means before a messenger comes, people have
a legitimate excuse for not believing. After
a messenger comes, that excuse is removed —
because they now have access to the message.

This is an epistemic standard built into the
Quran's own theology. Culpability requires
the removal of the legitimate excuse. The
legitimate excuse is removed by giving people
genuine epistemic access to the truth.

If Jews and Christians lacked a non-circular
way to identify Muhammad from corrupted
scriptures — they retained their *hujja*.
They had a legitimate excuse. The Quran
cannot simultaneously say they had no
legitimate excuse AND make it impossible
for them to have had genuine epistemic access.

**Surah 17:15:**
> وَمَا كُنَّا مُعَذِّبِينَ حَتَّىٰ نَبْعَثَ رَسُولًا
>
> "And We do not punish until We have sent
> a messenger."

Again — punishment and culpability require
the prior sending of a messenger, which
is a form of providing epistemic access.
Allah himself, in his own Quran, ties
moral accountability to the provision
of accessible evidence.

The epistemic access requirement is not
Western philosophy. It is Quranic theology.

### The Specific Epistemic Requirements the Quran Creates

The Quran does not just require generic
epistemic access. It requires three very
specific things:

**Requirement 1 — Non-Circularity:**
The evidence must be genuinely external
to the conclusion being evaluated. A Jew
evaluating Muhammad cannot be required
to already accept Muhammad's prophethood
in order to use the evidence that is
supposedly proving Muhammad's prophethood.
That is not evidence — that is coercion
dressed as evidence.

**Requirement 2 — Public Accessibility:**
The evidence must be available to the
community being held responsible, in a
form they can actually access and evaluate.
Not available in principle. Available in
practice — to real people, in a real
historical moment.

**Requirement 3 — Sufficient Discrimination:**
The evidence must be precise enough to
actually identify Muhammad — not just
point toward "a prophet" vaguely, but
identify Muhammad specifically. Because
the Quran's claim is specific: it says
Jews recognized *him* — this particular
person — with certainty.

Under the corruption model, none of
these three requirements can be met
without circularity. We will demonstrate
this exhaustively under Premises 6-12.

## Definition 5 — Prophetic Continuity

### What the Claim Is

Prophetic continuity is one of the two
load-bearing pillars of Islamic theology.
The other is monotheism (*tawhid*). Prophetic
continuity is the claim that Islam is not
a new religion appearing from nowhere —
it is the completion, restoration, and
culmination of the same divine revelation
that began with Adam, continued through
Abraham, Moses, David, and Jesus, and
was finally sealed in Muhammad.

This is not a peripheral Islamic claim.
It is central to why:
- The Quran addresses Jews and Christians
  directly (because they share the same
  prophetic heritage)
- Muhammad claimed authority over Jewish
  and Christian communities
- The Quran says Jews and Christians should
  have recognized Muhammad (because he
  stands in the line they already belong to)
- The Quran condemns their rejection as
  particularly grave (because they rejected
  someone they knew was coming)

Without prophetic continuity — Islam is
just another religion making claims.
With prophetic continuity — Islam claims
to be the rightful heir of everything
the Abrahamic tradition built.

### The Quran Anchors Continuity Externally

This is the critical point that many miss.

Prophetic continuity is not a self-referential
claim in the Quran. The Quran does not just
say "Muhammad is a prophet in the same line
as Moses — because the Quran says so."
That would be circular and would have no
power over Jews and Christians who do not
accept the Quran.

Instead, the Quran anchors continuity
**externally** — in the prior scriptures,
in the testimony of prior communities,
in texts that exist independently of
the Quran's own claims.

**Surah 46:12:**
> وَمِن قَبْلِهِ كِتَابُ مُوسَىٰ إِمَامًا
> وَرَحْمَةً ۚ وَهَٰذَا كِتَابٌ مُّصَدِّقٌ
> لِّسَانًا عَرَبِيًّا
>
> "Before it was the scripture of Moses
> as a guide and mercy. And this Book
> confirms it in the Arabic tongue."

The Quran presents itself as confirmed
by — and confirming — the Torah of Moses.
The relationship is bidirectional. The
Torah confirms the Quran; the Quran
confirms the Torah. This only works if
the Torah is intact enough to serve as
confirmation.

**Surah 10:94:**
> فَإِن كُنتَ فِي شَكٍّ مِّمَّا أَنزَلْنَا
> إِلَيْكَ فَاسْأَلِ الَّذِينَ يَقْرَءُونَ
> الْكِتَابَ مِن قَبْلِكَ
>
> "If you are in doubt about what We have
> revealed to you — ask those who have
> been reading the Book before you."

Allah tells Muhammad — directly — that
the verification mechanism for the Quran's
own claims is the prior scriptures as
read by those who have them. This is
Allah himself establishing that the
prior scriptures are the external anchor
for Quranic truth claims.

If those scriptures are corrupted:
- The external anchor is gone
- The only remaining verification is
  internal — the Quran verifying itself
- Which is circular
- Which means the Quran, by the standard
  its own God established, cannot be
  verified

**Surah 26:196-197:**
> وَإِنَّهُ لَفِي زُبُرِ الْأَوَّلِينَ ۝

## Definition 6 — Non-Circular Criterion

### What Circularity Is

A circular argument is one in which the
conclusion is assumed — explicitly or
implicitly — in one of the premises
used to support it.

Classic example:
- "The Bible is true because it is the
  Word of God."
- "How do you know it is the Word of God?"
- "Because the Bible says so."

The conclusion (the Bible is true) is
assumed in the premise used to support
it (the Bible says it is the Word of God
— which only matters if the Bible is true).

Circularity does not prove that a claim
is false. It proves that the argument
provided is not evidence for the claim.
A circular argument provides zero
evidential support for its conclusion
because it assumes what it is trying to prove.

### Why Non-Circularity Is Required Here

The Muslim case for Muhammad's prophethood —
specifically the external verification arm
of that case — requires non-circularity.

Here is why: The Quran's entire evidential
strategy for convincing Jews and Christians
is external. It does not say "believe because
you feel it." It says "your own scriptures
confirm this." This strategy only works if
the scriptures can be used as independent
external evidence — evidence that does not
already assume Muhammad's prophethood.

The moment any step in the verification
process assumes Muhammad's prophethood —
the external evidence strategy collapses.
You are no longer presenting evidence
to a skeptic. You are demanding that
the skeptic already believe your
conclusion before they can evaluate
your evidence. That is not an
evidential argument — it is coercion
dressed as argument.

### How to Identify Circular Criteria

A simple test: Ask whether a person who
**does not already accept Islam** could
use this criterion to non-circularly
evaluate Muhammad's prophethood.

- **Using the Quran to sort the Torah:**
  A non-Muslim cannot use the Quran to
  sort the Torah because accepting the
  Quran's sorting decisions requires
  accepting the Quran's authority —
  which requires accepting Muhammad.
  *Circular.*

- **Using Muhammad's sunnah to interpret
  the Torah:** A non-Muslim cannot use
  Muhammad's interpretations as a reliable
  guide because the reliability of his
  interpretations is exactly what is
  being evaluated. *Circular.*

- **Using a pro-Muhammad scholarly
  tradition to verify Muhammad:**
  A non-Muslim would rightly ask why
  they should privilege the tradition
  that accepts Muhammad over the tradition
  that rejects him — without a non-circular
  reason for the preference. *Circular.*

- **Using historical-critical reconstruction
  to identify the authentic Torah:**
  This is potentially non-circular — but
  it was not available in the 7th century.
  And when modern historical-critical
  scholarship is applied, it does not
  identify Muhammad in the Hebrew Bible.
  *Non-circular but unavailable and
  unhelpful to the Muslim.*

- **Using the prior theological framework
  of the Hebrew Bible to interpret it:**
  This is non-circular — the interpretive
  framework pre-exists and is independent
  of Muhammad. But when applied consistently,
  the theological categories of the Hebrew
  Bible do not produce Muhammad as their
  intended reference. *Non-circular but
  counter-productive for the Muslim.*

Every non-circular path leads away from
Muhammad. Every path toward Muhammad
is circular. This is not accidental.
We will build this out fully in
Premises 6-12.

## Premise 1 — Corruption Requires a Sorting Criterion

This premise does not make a religious claim.

Consider an analogy. Suppose a historian

- This ancient document is partially forged
- Some sections are authentic and some are
  fabrications inserted by later scribes

- And ancient scholars who had this document
  recognized that figure from it with certainty
- And those scholars are blameworthy for
  denying his identity

The moment you accept that the document is
partially forged — you know that reading it
straight to establish any claim requires a
prior criterion for sorting authentic from
forged content. Otherwise, the "description"
of the figure might be in the forged section.
The "recognition" might be of a forged passage.
The "blameworthiness" might be grounded in
something a forger invented.

This is not a philosophical imposition.
It is what partially forged means.

To deny Premise 1, the Muslim must claim
that a corrupted text can be read reliably
without any external sorting criterion.

Therefore the condition requiring a

Therefore the conditions for Islam

Therefore Islam is false.

## Premise 6 — Quran-Based Criteria Are Circular

Step 3: Muhammad's prophethood confirms

Step 4: The Quran's authority justifies

grounded. The entire system is self-referential.

- Its authority is the very thing under question
- Being told "use the Quran to identify

  the Quran"
- This provides zero non-circular evidence

The Quran itself seems aware of this problem —
which is precisely why Surah 10:94 directs
Muhammad to the prior scriptures for
verification, rather than saying "verify
the Quran by the Quran." The external
anchor was intentionally established to
avoid this exact circularity.

Using the Quran to sort the Torah collapses
that external anchor entirely.

### The Counter External Verification Argument — Full Expansion

**Sub-Premise 9a — What verification actually verifies:**

External verification of a text is verification
of its propositional content. Let us be very
precise about what this means.

When we say a prophecy was "fulfilled" — we
are saying that the state of affairs the
prophecy described actually came to pass.
We are verifying that a proposition — the
proposition expressed by the prophecy — turned
out to be true.

But here is the critical point: truth and
falsity are properties of PROPOSITIONS —
not of written marks on a page, not of
sounds in the air, not of ink on parchment.
The ink marks are just physical objects.
They become truth-apt only when we know
what propositions they express.

This matters because:
- Before you can verify whether a prophecy
  was fulfilled — you must first know what
  the prophecy said
- Knowing what the prophecy said requires
  knowing what propositions it expressed
- Knowing what propositions it expressed
  requires interpreting it correctly

You cannot verify the truth of something
you have not yet understood.

**Sub-Premise 9b — You cannot verify propositional
content without first identifying the propositions:**

Imagine I show you a text in classical
Akkadian cuneiform. I say: "This is a
prophecy that was fulfilled." Can you
verify whether it was fulfilled?

You cannot. Not because the text has no
meaning. But because you do not yet know
what propositions it expresses. Before
verification can begin — interpretation
must be complete.

This is not a controversial philosophical
claim. It is a basic epistemic fact about
how language and verification work. Every
Muslim who has ever read the Torah knows
this — because they interpret Torah passages
before claiming they point to Muhammad.
The interpretation comes first. The
verification claim comes after.

**Sub-Premise 9c — Interpretation requires context:**

Texts do not interpret themselves. Every
interpretation requires:
- Knowledge of the language in which the
  text was written
- Knowledge of the historical context in
  which it was produced
- Knowledge of the literary conventions
  the author used
- Knowledge of the theological framework
  within which the text was composed

For the Hebrew Bible, this means:
- Biblical Hebrew — with its specific
  semantic ranges, idioms, and grammatical
  structures
- The historical context of ancient Israel —
  the specific political, religious, and
  cultural world in which the texts were
  produced
- The literary conventions of ancient
  Near Eastern prophetic literature
- The theological framework of ancient
  Israelite religion — what concepts, categories,
  and expectations were operative for the
  original authors and communities

**Sub-Premise 9d — Interpretation is constrained
by prior theology:**

This is the crucial sub-premise. The meaning
of a text is not free-floating. It is anchored
in the conceptual framework of the community
that produced it. The theological categories
operative in the originating community
constrain which interpretations are admissible.

This is not a contentious philosophical
position. It is how every credible ancient
