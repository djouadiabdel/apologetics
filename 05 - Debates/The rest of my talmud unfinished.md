2 — The Minimal Facts Argument

Gary Habermas's approach — accepted by the majority of secular historians regardless of their position on Christianity:

Five facts virtually all historians — atheist, agnostic, Jewish, secular — accept:

    Jesus died by crucifixion

    The disciples believed they saw him risen

    Paul converted after what he described as an appearance

    James converted after what he described as an appearance

    The tomb was empty

The challenge: Produce a naturalistic explanation that accounts for all five simultaneously better than the resurrection does.

Every alternative theory — hallucination, swoon, theft, wrong tomb — fails on at least two or three of the five. The resurrection accounts for all five in one.
3 — The New Testament Manuscript Evidence

We never touched the textual reliability question:

    Over 5,800 Greek manuscripts of the New Testament — more than any other ancient document by enormous margin

    Homer's Iliad — second most attested ancient document — 1,800 manuscripts

    Caesar's Gallic Wars — 10 manuscripts, oldest copy 1,000 years after original

    The New Testament oldest manuscripts date to within decades of the original writings — not centuries

    The gap between original writing and oldest surviving copy for the NT is the smallest of any ancient document

If you reject the New Testament on manuscript grounds — you must reject every ancient historical document we have by the same standard. Nobody does this consistently.
4 — Archaeology Confirming the New Testament

The New Testament is remarkably accurate on historical details that were only verified by archaeology centuries later:

    The Pool of Bethesda — John 5 — dismissed as legendary by scholars until discovered in the 19th century exactly as described

    The Pool of Siloam — John 9 — discovered in 2004

    Pontius Pilate — a stone inscription bearing his name discovered in Caesarea Maritima in 1961 — confirming the title Prefect of Judea exactly as the Gospels use

    The ossuary of Caiaphas — the high priest who condemned Jesus — discovered in 1990

    John 19:13 — the Pavement (Gabbatha) — identified archaeologically

    Luke's census of Quirinius — long mocked as historically wrong — now understood as referencing a different administrative event Luke accurately distinguished

Luke in particular — the author of both Luke and Acts — has been verified as historically accurate on over 80 specific details of geography, titles, customs, and political arrangements by classical archaeologist Colin Hemer.
5 — The Argument From Consciousness

We never touched philosophy of mind:

    Materialism — the view that consciousness is purely physical — faces the hard problem of consciousness — why does subjective experience exist at all if everything is just atoms

    The existence of consciousness is more naturally explained by a universe created by a conscious being than by blind physical processes

    Thomas Nagel — an atheist philosopher — wrote Mind and Cosmos arguing that materialism cannot explain consciousness and that a teleological account of reality is necessary

    This is not a God-of-the-gaps argument — it is a philosophical argument from the nature of consciousness itself

6 — The Fine-Tuning Argument

We never touched cosmology:

    The fundamental constants of the universe — the gravitational constant, the cosmological constant, the strong nuclear force — are tuned to extraordinary precision for life to exist

    The cosmological constant is fine-tuned to one part in ten to the power of 120 — the most precise number in all of physics

    If any of these constants varied by tiny amounts — no stars, no planets, no chemistry, no life — nothing

    This is not a religious claim — it is acknowledged by atheist physicists including Steven Weinberg and Leonard Susskind

    The multiverse is proposed to explain it — but the multiverse is currently unfalsifiable and requires its own explanation for why a life-permitting universe exists in it
