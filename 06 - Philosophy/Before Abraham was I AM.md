"I AM" — The Divine Name in Scripture
The Hebrew Origin: אֶהְיֶה (Ehyeh)
The foundational moment is Exodus 3:14, when Moses asks God His name and God replies:

"אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה" — Ehyeh Asher Ehyeh
Translated: "I AM THAT I AM" or "I WILL BE WHAT I WILL BE"


Ehyeh (אֶהְיֶה) comes from the Hebrew root הָיָה (hayah) — meaning "to be," "to exist," "to become"
It is the first person singular form — I am / I exist / I will be
God then shortens it: "Tell them EHYEH sent me" — essentially "I AM" is God's self-given name


The Connection to YHWH (יהוה)
This is the critical link. The name YHWH (the Tetragrammaton — the 4-letter name of God) is the third person form of the same root hayah:
FormHebrewMeaningGod speaking of HimselfEhyehI AM / I existOthers speaking of GodYHWHHe IS / He who causes to be
So YHWH essentially means "He Who IS" or "The Self-Existent One" — the one who simply is, with no beginning, no end, no outside cause.

The Greek: ἐγώ εἰμι (Ego Eimi)
When the Hebrew scriptures were translated into Greek (the Septuagint / LXX, ~3rd century BC), Ehyeh Asher Ehyeh was rendered:

ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ὤν — "I am the Being / I am the One Who Is"


Ego = I
Eimi = I am (from the verb einai — "to be")

This is enormously significant in the New Testament, because Jesus uses "Ego Eimi" repeatedly and deliberately:

"Before Abraham was, I AM" — John 8:58 (the crowd immediately tried to stone Him — they understood exactly what He was claiming)
"I AM the way, the truth, and the life" — John 14:6
"I AM the bread of life" — John 6:35
"I AM the resurrection" — John 11:25
In Gethsemane when soldiers came for Him, He said "I AM" and they fell backward to the ground — John 18:6


The Deeper Theological Meaning
The name carries layers of meaning:

Eternal Self-Existence — God doesn't have existence, He IS existence itself. Everything else exists because of Him. He alone exists from Himself (aseity).
Unchanging Constancy — "I will be what I will be" also means He is completely consistent — what He was, He is, He will always be. (Hebrews 13:8 — "Jesus Christ, the same yesterday, today, and forever")
Uncontainable Nature — Some scholars argue the vagueness is intentional. "I am who I am" is also a way of saying no name can fully contain Me — I exist beyond all categories.
Present Tense Permanence — Not "I was" or "I will be" — but I AM. Always present. Always active. (Revelation 1:8 — "I am the Alpha and Omega... who is, and who was, and who is to come")


Summary Chain
הָיָה (hayah — Hebrew root: "to be")
→ אֶהְיֶה (Ehyeh — "I AM," God's self-name, Exodus 3:14)
→ יהוה (YHWH — "He IS," the name others call God)
→ ἐγώ εἰμι (Ego Eimi — Greek "I AM," used by Jesus in John)
→ All pointing to the same truth: God is the ground of all being — existence itself.
