## The Claim in One Sentence

When Jesus said "Before Abraham was, I AM" (John 8:58), he was not using
bad grammar — he was deliberately invoking the divine name of God from
Exodus 3:14, and the crowd understood it immediately. That is why they
picked up stones.

---

## Step 1 — The Hebrew Origin: אֶהְיֶה (Ehyeh)

The foundational moment is Exodus 3:14. Moses asks God His name:

> "אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה" — *Ehyeh Asher Ehyeh*  
> "I AM THAT I AM" / "I WILL BE WHAT I WILL BE"

- **Ehyeh** (אֶהְיֶה) comes from the root הָיָה (*hayah*) — "to be, to exist"
- It is first person singular — *I am / I exist*
- God then shortens it: "Tell them **EHYEH** sent me" — I AM is God's own name

---

## Step 2 — The Connection to YHWH (יהוה)

YHWH (the Tetragrammaton) is the **third person form** of the same root:

| Form | Hebrew | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| God speaking of Himself | אֶהְיֶה Ehyeh | I AM / I exist |
| Others speaking of God | יהוה YHWH | He IS / He who causes to be |

YHWH means "He Who IS" — the Self-Existent One. No beginning, no end,
no outside cause. Existence itself.

---

## Step 3 — The Greek: ἐγώ εἰμι (Ego Eimi)

When the Hebrew scriptures were translated into Greek (the Septuagint,
~3rd century BC), Ehyeh Asher Ehyeh became:

> **ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ὤν** — "I am the Being / I am the One Who Is"

- *Ego* = I
- *Eimi* = I am (from *einai* — to be)

This is the exact phrase Jesus uses in the New Testament — **deliberately**:

- **"Before Abraham was, I AM"** — John 8:58  
  *(The crowd immediately tried to stone Him — they understood perfectly)*
- "I AM the way, the truth, and the life" — John 14:6
- "I AM the bread of life" — John 6:35
- "I AM the resurrection" — John 11:25
- In Gethsemane: soldiers came for Him, He said "I AM" — they fell
  backward to the ground (John 18:6)

---

## Step 4 — The Theological Depth

The name carries four layers:

1. **Eternal Self-Existence (Aseity)** — God doesn't *have* existence,
   He *IS* existence. Everything else exists because of Him. He alone
   exists from Himself.

2. **Unchanging Constancy** — "I will be what I will be" means complete
   consistency. What He was, He is, He always will be.
   > "Jesus Christ, the same yesterday, today, and forever." — Hebrews 13:8

3. **Uncontainable Nature** — The vagueness is intentional. "I am who I
   am" means: no name can fully contain Me. I exist beyond all categories.

4. **Present Tense Permanence** — Not "I was" or "I will be" — but I AM.
   Always present. Always active.
   > "Who is, and who was, and who is to come." — Revelation 1:8
