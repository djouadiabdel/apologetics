Original Sin — The Complete Case Including Islamic Parallels
What Original Sin Is NOT

Before building the positive case, eliminate the caricature — because the Muslim objection almost always attacks a version of original sin that most Christians do not hold.

The caricature: Every human being is born guilty of Adam's personal sin and condemned to hell for something they did not personally do. Infants who die unbaptized go to hell.

What classical Christianity actually teaches:

    Humanity inherited a corrupted nature — a bent, a proclivity, a structural tendency toward rebellion

    Humanity inherited the consequences of Adam's sin — death, suffering, moral weakness

    Humanity does not inherit Adam's personal guilt in the sense of being condemned for his specific act

    Moral accountability begins when a person can distinguish good from evil — not at birth

The Eastern Orthodox tradition makes this explicit: they affirm ancestral sin (inherited corruption and death) while rejecting inherited guilt. This is not a liberal revision — it is the dominant pre-Augustinian understanding of the church.
The Hebrew Bible Case — Systematically

Genesis 6:5
"The Lord saw that the wickedness of man was great in the earth, and that every intention of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually."

Not some men. Not occasionally. Every intention. Only evil. Continually. This is a structural diagnosis of human nature — not a description of individual bad choices.

Genesis 8:21
After the flood, God says:
"I will never again curse the ground because of man, for the intention of man's heart is evil from his youth."

Notice: God acknowledges the permanent condition and chooses mercy despite it — not because humans have improved. The evil inclination is assumed as a fixed feature of human nature from youth.

Job 14:4
"Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? There is not one."

The logic is generational and ontological — not just behavioral. An unclean source cannot produce a clean product. The corruption is transmitted through the human line.

Job 15:14-16
"What is man, that he can be pure? Or he who is born of a woman, that he can be righteous? Behold, God puts no trust in his holy ones, and the heavens are not pure in his sight; how much less one who is abominable and corrupt, a man who drinks injustice like water."

"Drinks injustice like water" — injustice is not an external temptation resisted. It is consumed naturally, effortlessly, as a basic appetite. This is precisely what original sin describes — sin as second nature, not merely bad choices.

Job 25:4-6
"How then can man be in the right before God? How can he who is born of a woman be pure? Behold, even the moon is not bright, and the stars are not pure in his eyes; how much less man, who is a maggot, and the son of man, who is a worm!"

Ecclesiastes 7:20
"Surely there is not a righteous man on earth who does good and never sins."

Not occasionally sins. Not sins under extreme pressure. Never sins — the negation is absolute. Universal moral failure is the condition of every human being.

Ecclesiastes 7:29
"See, this alone I found, that God made man upright, but they have sought out many schemes."

This is the precise structure of the fall doctrine:

    God created man good (upright)

    Man corrupted himself through his own choices (sought out many schemes)

    The result is a corrupted nature passed down

Ecclesiastes 9:3
"The hearts of the children of man are full of evil, and madness is in their hearts while they live."

Jeremiah 17:9
"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately sick; who can understand it?"

The Hebrew word translated "desperately sick" is 'anush — used elsewhere for incurable illness, terminal conditions. Jeremiah is not describing temporary moral failure. He is describing a disease of the heart that is beyond natural cure.
Paul's Systematic Treatment — Romans 5 and 7

Romans 5:12
"Therefore, just as sin came into the world through one man, and death through sin, and so death spread to all men because all sinned."

The transmission line is explicit: one man → sin enters the world → death through sin → death spreads to all. Paul is not saying all people commit the same sin as Adam. He is saying Adam's sin introduced a condition — mortality, corruption, the bondage of the will — that became universal.

Romans 5:19
"For as by the one man's disobedience the many were made sinners, so by the one man's obedience the many will be made righteous."

Made sinners — not merely influenced, not merely given a bad example. The Greek kathistemi means constituted, appointed, set in a condition. Adam's disobedience constituted humanity in a state of sinfulness.

Romans 7:14-24
Paul's most personal description of the condition:

"I am fleshly, sold under sin... For I do not do what I want, but I do the very thing I hate... It is no longer I who do it, but sin that dwells within me... I find it to be a law that when I want to do right, evil lies close at hand... Wretched man that I am! Who will deliver me from this body of death?"

The language is not of bad habits or weak willpower. Paul describes sin as an indwelling agent — a power operating through the flesh independently of his own will. This is precisely the doctrine: not just that humans sin, but that there is something in humans that drives them toward sin against their own better judgment.
The Accountability Question — When Does It Begin?

This is where the Islamic objection usually focuses: "How can a baby be born sinful? That is unjust."

The answer from scripture is that inherited nature and moral accountability are separate questions.

Deuteronomy 1:39
God spares the children of the wilderness generation:
"Your little ones, who you said would become a prey, and your children, who today have no knowledge of good or evil, shall go in there."

The criterion for entering the promised land — and by extension for moral accountability — is knowledge of good and evil. The children lacked it. Therefore they were not held to the same judgment as their parents.

Isaiah 7:15-16
Speaking of a child:
"He shall eat curds and honey when he knows how to refuse the evil and choose the good. For before the boy knows how to refuse the evil and choose the good..."

Again: moral knowledge is developmental. Before it arrives, the child is not accountable.

Romans 4:15
"For the law brings wrath, but where there is no law there is no transgression."

Romans 5:13
"For sin indeed was in the world before the law was given, but sin is not counted where there is no law."

Sin exists in human nature universally. But it is not counted — not held against a person — until the law (moral awareness) is present. The infant has the nature. The infant does not yet have the accountability.

Matthew 18:1-5; 19:13-15
Jesus says the kingdom belongs to children and that adults must become like children to enter it. If children were born condemned, Jesus's use of them as the model of kingdom citizenship would be incoherent.
The Islamic Parallel — Fitra and Nafs al-Ammara

This is where the argument becomes devastating — because Islam has its own structural equivalent of original sin and most Muslims do not realize it.

Fitra — The Corrupted Original Nature

The Quran and Hadith teach that every human being is born with fitra — the original pure nature inclined toward God (Islam). And yet every human being deviates from it.

Surah 30:30:
"So direct your face toward the religion, inclining to truth. Adhere to the fitra of Allah upon which He has created all people. No change should there be in the creation of Allah."

The Prophet said in Sahih al-Bukhari (1385):
"Every child is born on the fitra. Then his parents make him a Jew, a Christian, or a Zoroastrian."

The problem this creates for Islam:

If every human is born with a pure nature inclined toward God — why does every human being deviate from it? The Hadith blames the parents. But who corrupted the first parents? And who corrupted their parents?

Islam has no structural answer to why the corruption is universal. It asserts fitra without explaining the universal failure of fitra. Christianity's answer — that the nature was corrupted at the source, in Adam — is the only explanation that accounts for the universality of the deviation.

Nafs al-Ammara — The Soul That Commands Evil

The Quran explicitly teaches that the human soul has a component that commands toward evil:

Surah 12:53 (Yusuf/Joseph):
"And I do not acquit myself. Indeed, the soul is a persistent enjoiner of evil, except those upon which my Lord has mercy."

Nafs al-ammara bil-su' — the soul that persistently commands toward evil. This is not describing temporary temptation. It is describing a structural feature of the human soul that persistently drives toward sin.

This is original sin in Islamic vocabulary. The human soul has an internal orientation toward evil that requires divine mercy to overcome. That is precisely what Christianity means by the sinful nature inherited from Adam.

Surah 4:28
"Allah wants to lighten your burdens, for man was created weak."

Man was created weak — da'if. Not circumstantially weak. Constitutionally weak. Built with a weakness toward temptation as a feature of human nature. This is not the description of a being born pure who simply makes bad choices. This is the description of a being whose nature has a structural vulnerability.

Surah 33:72
"Indeed, We offered the Trust to the heavens and the earth and the mountains, and they declined to bear it and feared it; but man undertook to bear it. Indeed, he was unjust and ignorant."

Man took on the burden of moral responsibility — and the Quran's verdict is that he was unjust and ignorant in doing so. The tendency toward injustice and ignorance is presented as a characteristic of the human being.

Sahih Muslim — The Shaytan and Every Child

The Prophet Muhammad said (Sahih Muslim 2366):
"There is none born among the offspring of Adam but Satan touches it. A child therefore, cries loudly at the time of birth because of the touch of Satan, except Mary and her child."

This is remarkable. Every human child born is touched by Satan at the moment of birth — with the sole exception being Jesus and Mary. Islam teaches that at the very moment of entry into the world, every human being is marked by Satanic contact.

This is not identical to original sin — but it is functionally equivalent. A universal corruption affecting every human being from the first moment of life, transmitted through the human line, with one miraculous exception. The structure is identical to what Christianity claims. The vocabulary differs. The underlying claim is the same.
