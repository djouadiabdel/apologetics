## The Core Argument (William Lane Craig)

**P1.** If God does not exist, objective moral values and duties do not exist.  
**P2.** Objective moral values and duties do exist.  
**C.**  Therefore, God exists.

This is a *modus tollens* — logically valid. To defeat it, your opponent
must deny P1 or P2. Both are extremely costly moves.

---

## Why P1 Is True

In a universe without God, what exists? Matter, energy, space, time —
and the blind processes of natural selection.

Natural selection does not care about goodness. It cares about survival
and reproduction. A lioness that eats her cubs when resources are scarce
is doing exactly what evolution "approves of." There is no fact of the
matter — on pure naturalism — that says she did something *wrong*.

Without God:
- Morality reduces to evolved preferences — no different in status from a
  preference for sugar over salt
- "Torturing children for fun is wrong" becomes equivalent to "I dislike
  the taste of spinach" — a personal or cultural feeling, not a fact
- Human beings have no more objective worth than cockroaches — we are all
  just accidental arrangements of atoms
- Moral "oughts" become incoherent — matter cannot *obligate* you to do
  anything

As Friedrich Nietzsche — an atheist — honestly admitted:
> "There are no moral facts whatsoever."

If atheism is true, Nietzsche is right. Most atheists are not willing to
follow their worldview to its honest conclusion.

## Why P2 Is True

You already know this. Consider:

- The Holocaust was not merely *unpopular* — it was *wrong*
- Raping a child is not just something most people *dislike* — it is
  *objectively evil*
- Sacrificing one innocent person to entertain a crowd is *wrong*,
  regardless of whether the crowd enjoys it

These are not cultural preferences. If someone said "the Holocaust was
morally fine — I just personally disagree with it," we would not say they
had a different opinion. We would say they were *wrong* — and we would
mean it objectively.

The moral experience of humanity is near-universal: some things are really
wrong. Not just inconvenient. Not just unpopular. *Wrong.*

Denying P2 is more costly than accepting God. It requires you to say:
> "The Holocaust was not objectively evil. It was just a different
> cultural preference."

No serious person actually believes this. Which means almost everyone
already implicitly accepts P2.

## The Euthyphro Dilemma (The Main Objection)

This is the oldest and most common objection. From Plato's dialogue:

> "Is something good because God commands it — or does God command it
> because it is good?"

**Horn 1:** If good = whatever God commands → God could command child
torture and it would be "good." Morality becomes arbitrary.

**Horn 2:** If God commands things because they are already good →
goodness exists independently of God. God becomes unnecessary.

### The Third Option (The Standard Christian Response)

The dilemma is a **false dilemma**. It assumes only two options. There is
a third: [web:755][web:761]

> **Goodness is not above God, nor arbitrarily decided by God — it is
> identical to God's nature.**

God does not *consult* a moral standard above himself. God does not
*arbitrarily* invent morality. God *is* the standard. His nature is
essentially and necessarily good — and his commands flow from that nature.

- This avoids Horn 1: morality is not arbitrary — it is anchored in God's
  unchanging nature
- This avoids Horn 2: goodness is not independent of God — God *is*
  goodness itself

Aquinas: God is not *subject* to the Good. God *is* the Good. *Ipsum
Bonum* — Goodness itself.

## The Atheist's Own Dilemma

The Euthyphro dilemma can be flipped:

> "Is something good because evolution selected for it — or is it good
> independently of evolution?"

**Horn 1:** If good = whatever evolution selected → rape, infanticide, and
tribal genocide are "good" when they aid survival. This is monstrous.

**Horn 2:** If good exists independently of evolution → you have just
admitted that objective moral facts exist *beyond* the physical universe.
Now you need to explain what grounds them. You are back to needing God.

The atheist faces the exact same dilemma they throw at us — and has no
third option. [web:754][web:759]

## Objections and Responses

### "Evolution explains morality."
**Response:** Evolution explains *why we have moral feelings*. It does not
explain why those feelings track anything *real*. We evolved a fear of
spiders — that doesn't make spiders objectively terrifying. We evolved
empathy — that doesn't make empathy objectively *binding*.

Evolution gives you moral *psychology*. It cannot give you moral *facts*.

### "We can be good without God."
**Response:** Completely agreed — and the argument never claims otherwise.
The argument is not about *motivation* (can people behave morally without
believing in God?) — it is about *grounding* (what makes moral claims
*objectively true*?).

An atheist can be a wonderful, moral person. But on atheism, their
goodness is ultimately just a preference — like preferring jazz to pop.
There is no fact of the matter that they are *right*.

### "Human flourishing grounds morality."
**Response:** Why does human flourishing *matter* objectively? On
naturalism, humans are just one animal among millions. Why does our
flourishing have cosmic moral weight that a cockroach's doesn't? You need
a reason to privilege human beings — and that reason points back to us
being made in the image of something that has intrinsic value: God.

### "Secular humanism grounds morality."
**Response:** Secular humanism *asserts* human dignity. It does not
*ground* it. Asserting that humans matter does not make them matter
objectively in a universe of blind matter and energy. You are borrowing
the conclusion without the premise.

## The Islam-Specific Application

Muslims agree that objective moral values exist — the Quran affirms it
constantly. This means Muslims implicitly accept P2.

The debate with Muslims is therefore not about whether God grounds
morality — both sides agree he does. The debate is about *which God* and
*which revelation*.

Key move: The moral argument is *shared ground* with Muslims. Use it to
close off atheism and secular alternatives together, then shift to:
- Why the Quran's moral framework has internal problems (slavery, sex
  captives, hudud)
- Why the Christian framework — grounded in the Imago Dei and the
  Incarnation — provides a more coherent basis for universal human dignity

## Debate Bullets (Quick Use)

- "Was the Holocaust objectively wrong — or just something you personally
  dislike? If objectively wrong, what makes it so in a universe of blind
  matter?"
- "Nietzsche was an atheist and he was honest: no God means no moral
  facts. Are you more consistent than Nietzsche?"
- "Evolution explains why we *feel* moral. It cannot explain why we *are*
  obligated. Those are completely different questions."
- "You say humans have dignity. Why? In a universe of atoms, why do
  *your* atoms matter more than a cockroach's?"
- "The Euthyphro dilemma cuts both ways. Is something good because
  evolution selected it? If yes, rape that aids survival is good. If no,
  you already believe in objective moral facts beyond nature."
