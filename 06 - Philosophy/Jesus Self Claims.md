## The Core Question

Did Jesus claim to be God? Muslims and skeptics say no — he was just a
prophet, a moral teacher, a wise man. The Gospels say otherwise. This
file catalogs every major self-claim of Jesus, shows what they meant to
a 1st century Jewish audience, and explains why the "just a prophet"
reading is historically incoherent.

---

## Category 1 — The Divine Name Claims

### "Before Abraham Was, I AM" — John 8:58
The most explicit claim. See [[Before Abraham was I AM]] for the full
treatment.

- Jesus uses *Ego Eimi* — the Greek rendering of God's name from Exodus 3:14
- He switches tenses deliberately: Abraham *came into being* (aorist),
  but Jesus *I AM* (present eternal)
- The crowd's response: immediate attempt to stone Him for blasphemy

### "I and the Father Are One" — John 10:30
- Jews picked up stones immediately (v.31)
- Jesus asks why — they answer: "For blasphemy — because you, a mere man,
  claim to be God" (v.33)
- Jesus does not correct them — he doubles down and defends the claim

### The Seven "I AM" Statements in John
Jesus applies the divine name to himself seven times with explicit
predicates — each one a claim to divine identity:

| Statement | Reference | What It Claims |
|---|---|---|
| I AM the Bread of Life | John 6:35 | Source of eternal sustenance |
| I AM the Light of the World | John 8:12 | Creator function (Genesis 1) |
| I AM the Gate | John 10:9 | Only access to God |
| I AM the Good Shepherd | John 10:11 | YHWH's title in Ezekiel 34 |
| I AM the Resurrection and the Life | John 11:25 | Authority over death |
| I AM the Way, Truth, and Life | John 14:6 | Exclusive path to the Father |
| I AM the True Vine | John 15:1 | Israel's role transferred to himself |

In Gethsemane (John 18:6) — soldiers come to arrest Him. He says
"I AM" (*Ego Eimi*) with no predicate — raw divine name — and they
fall backward to the ground.

## Category 2 — The Son of Man Claim

This is the title Jesus used **more than any other for himself** — over
80 times in the Gospels. It was not humility. It was a precise
theological claim. [web:784]

### The Source: Daniel 7:13-14
> "I saw in the night visions, and behold, one like the Son of Man came
> with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of Days..."

This figure:
- Is human in appearance ("Son of Man")
- Yet **rides the clouds** — in Jewish Scripture, only God rides clouds
  (Psalm 104:3, Isaiah 19:1, Nahum 1:3)
- Receives **eternal dominion** over all nations
- Is **served/worshipped** by all peoples

This is not a human figure. This is a divine ruler receiving worship.

### Jesus Quotes Daniel 7 at His Trial
At his trial the high priest asks: *"Are you the Christ, the Son of the
Blessed?"*

Jesus answers:
> "I am. And you will see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of
> Power, and coming with the clouds of heaven." — Mark 14:62

He quotes Daniel 7 **verbatim**. The high priest tears his robes —
the reaction for blasphemy. He understood exactly what Jesus claimed.

### Why This Title Is Devastating to the "Just a Prophet" View
Prophets do not claim Daniel 7. Prophets speak *about* God. Jesus places
himself *as* the figure who receives eternal worship alongside God.
No prophet in Jewish history did this. [web:784]

## Category 3 — Forgiving Sins

**Mark 2:1-11** — A paralyzed man is brought to Jesus. Jesus says:
> "Son, your sins are forgiven."

The scribes react immediately:
> "Why does this man speak like that? He is blaspheming! Who can forgive
> sins but God alone?"

Jesus does not say "you misunderstood me." He validates the miracle to
prove his authority to forgive sins — a divine prerogative. [web:780][web:786]

In Jewish theology, sin is ultimately an offense against God. Only God
can forgive sins against God. By forgiving sins, Jesus was claiming the
authority of God himself.

## Category 4 — Accepting Worship

Jewish law was unambiguous: worship belongs to God alone. Yet Jesus
**repeatedly accepts worship** and never corrects it:

- Thomas calls him "My Lord and my God" — John 20:28. Jesus accepts it.
- Disciples worship him after the resurrection — Matthew 28:17. Jesus accepts it.
- A healed blind man worships him — John 9:38. Jesus accepts it.
- He accepts worship in the boat — Matthew 14:33. Jesus accepts it.

Compare with what angels and apostles do when people try to worship them:

- Angel to John: "Do not do that! I am a fellow servant." — Revelation 22:9
- Peter to Cornelius: "Stand up. I myself am only a man." — Acts 10:26

Jesus never says this. He accepts what angels and apostles refuse. The
only coherent explanation is that he believed he deserved it.

## Category 5 — Authority Over Torah

A prophet says: **"Thus says the Lord."**
Jesus says: **"You have heard it said... but I say to you."**

In the Sermon on the Mount (Matthew 5-7) Jesus repeatedly places his
own authority **above Moses** — not alongside Moses, not interpreting
Moses, but superseding Moses:

- "Moses said... but I say to you" — 6 times in Matthew 5
- "I tell you the truth" (*Amen, amen lego hymin*) — his own authority
  formula, not referencing God

No Jewish prophet spoke this way. The prophets were messengers. Jesus
spoke as the **source**.

## Category 6 — Pre-existence Claims

- "Before Abraham was, I AM" — John 8:58
- "Father, glorify me in your presence with the glory I had with you
  **before the world began**" — John 17:5
- "No one has ascended into heaven except he who descended from heaven,
  the Son of Man" — John 3:13
- "I am the living bread that **came down from heaven**" — John 6:51

These are not claims of a prophet who was born in Bethlehem. They are
claims of pre-cosmic existence — existence before creation itself.

## The Lewis Trilemma

C.S. Lewis in *Mere Christianity* argued that given Jesus' claims, only
three options exist: [web:788][web:791]

> **Liar** — He knew he wasn't God and deceived people deliberately  
> **Lunatic** — He genuinely believed it but was delusional  
> **Lord** — His claims were true

### Why Liar Fails
- He died for the claim — liars don't die for known lies
- His ethical teaching is the most morally coherent in history — not the
  profile of a calculated deceiver
- His behavior under trial was dignified and consistent — not panicked

### Why Lunatic Fails
- Delusional people are incoherent and inconsistent
- Jesus' teaching shows extraordinary insight, internal consistency, and
  wisdom — the opposite of delusion
- His calmness under pressure, his handling of traps, his depth of
  teaching — none of this fits a madman

### Why "Legend" Fails (the 4th L — the common Muslim objection)
Muslims say: Jesus never actually claimed to be God — the Gospels are
corrupted legends written later.

Response:
- The earliest creed (1 Corinthians 15:3-7) dates to **within 2-5 years**
  of the crucifixion — see [[Is paulien creed pre-paulien?]]
- The Son of Man title is found across **all four Gospels and Q source** —
  it cannot be a late legendary addition
- The stoning attempts in John are not theological inventions — they are
  historically embarrassing details that a legend-maker would remove
- Bart Ehrman (not a Christian) agrees Jesus claimed a unique divine
  role — he just disputes what Jesus meant by it

## The Muslim "Just a Prophet" Argument Dismantled

**"Jesus never said 'I am God, worship me.'"**

Response: No 1st century Jewish teacher would say this in those exact
words — it would be incomprehensible to a monotheistic audience. Jesus
communicated divinity the way a 1st century Jew would: through divine
name claims, Daniel 7 language, forgiving sins, accepting worship, and
placing his authority above Torah. Every one of these was understood as
a divine claim — that is why they tried to stone him repeatedly.

**"The Gospel of John is late and unreliable."**

Response: The Synoptic Gospels (Mark, Matthew, Luke) also contain divine
claims — forgiving sins (Mark 2), Son of Man/Daniel 7 (Mark 14), authority
over Torah (Matthew 5). John adds depth but does not invent the claims.

**"Son of God just means a righteous human in Hebrew."**

Response: In some contexts yes — but not in the context of Mark 14:62
where Jesus quotes Daniel 7 at his trial and the high priest tears his
robes for blasphemy. The reaction tells you how they understood the claim.

## Debate Bullets

- "Why did they try to stone him after 'I and the Father are one'? You
  don't stone a prophet for claiming unity of purpose."
- "Why did the high priest tear his robes at the trial? That is the
  blasphemy response — not the response to a messianic claim."
- "Prophets say 'Thus says the Lord.' Jesus says 'But I say to you.'
  What prophet put their own words above God's Torah?"
- "Jesus accepted worship that angels refused. Either he was more
  deluded than any lunatic in history — or he was right."
- "The Son of Man title is in Daniel 7. That figure rides clouds, receives
  eternal dominion, and is worshipped by all nations. Jesus claimed to be
  that figure. That is not a prophet. That is God."
